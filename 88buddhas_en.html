The 88 Buddhas Repentance

With great kindness and compassion to pity living beings,
With great joy and equanimity to save all conscious life,
Adorned with the major marks and minor characteristics of light,
We all sincerely take refuge and pay homage.

Namo, we take refuge in all Buddhas of the ten directions and the extent of the dharma realms.
Namo, we take refuge in all honored Dharmas of the ten directions and the extent of the dharma realms.
Namo, we take refuge in all sage and noble Sanghas of the ten directions and the extent of the dharma realms.

Namo Tathagata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Knight, Taming and Regulating Hero, Teacher of Gods and Humans, Buddha, World Honored One.
Namo Amitabha Buddha, whose body is the treasury of the Dharma Realm.

All these World Honored Buddhas and others from all worlds, constantly abide in the world. May these World Honored Ones kindly be mindful of me. Whether in this life or previous lives, since beginningless birth and death, I have committed many offenses. Whether I did them myself, told others to do them, or rejoiced in seeing them done. Whether taking property of stupas, or of the Sangha, or of the Sanghas of the four directions. Whether I took it myself, told others to take it, or rejoiced in seeing it taken. Committing the five offenses of uninterrupted hell, whether I did them myself, told others to do them, or rejoiced in seeing them done. Committing the ten unwholesome paths, whether I did them myself, told others to do them, or rejoiced in seeing them done. The karmic obstacles created, whether concealed or not concealed, should cause me to fall into the hells, hungry ghosts, animals, or other evil destinies, to be born in borderlands or as a lowly person, or among barbarians. All such karmic obstacles, I now completely repent.

May all the World Honored Buddhas bear witness to me and be mindful of me. Before all World Honored Buddhas, I further speak these words: In this life or in past lives, any wholesome roots I have gained through practicing charity or observing pure precepts, even giving a morsel of food to an animal; any wholesome roots from practicing pure conduct; any wholesome roots from bringing living beings to accomplishment; any wholesome roots from cultivating Bodhi; and any wholesome roots from attaining unsurpassed wisdom. Gathering them all up, calculating and measuring them, I dedicate them all to Unsurpassed, Right, and Equal Enlightenment. Just as the Buddhas of the past, present, and future have dedicated, I too dedicate in the same way.

I repent of all sins, and rejoice in all blessings. I beseech the Buddhas for their merit and virtue, and vow to accomplish the unsurpassed wisdom. To the Buddhas of the past, present, and future, the most supreme among living beings, with oceans of infinite merit and virtue, I now dedicate my life and pay homage.
