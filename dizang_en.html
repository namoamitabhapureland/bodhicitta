Ksitigarbha's Vow Ocean

I. Harmony of Amitabha and Ksitigarbha
The Infinite Life Sutra points to the ultimate bliss of "no falling into the three lower realms," while Bodhisattva Ksitigarbha made the grand vow: "Until the hells are empty, I vow not to achieve Buddhahood." In the Dharmadhatu, these two seemingly contrast like Yin and Yang, yet harmonize perfectly. "Carrying karma to the Pure Land" perhaps does not mean past karma simply vanishes out of thin air. In the mundane world, who is truly without karma? However, Amitabha's vow unconditionally embraces all beings, utilizing the mechanism of a golden lotus body to physically transcend mortal dimensions. Thus, rebirth in the Pure Land relies entirely on Amitabha's vow, seemingly irrespective of one's karmic weight. Conversely, Ksitigarbha’s Dharma gate acts as a compassionate alternative within the karmic ocean—a sanctuary for eradicating karma and suffering for those unwilling or not yet ready to directly ascend to the Pure Land.
II. The Buddha's Entrustment
The transmission of Northern Buddhism often accompanies the return of Bodhisattvas through their vows. During the long, dark era after Sakyamuni Buddha's Nirvana and before Maitreya's descent, the Dharma in this Saha world seems to hang by a mere thread. In the Trayastrimsa Heaven, the Buddha deeply entrusted the heavy responsibility of saving this world to Ksitigarbha. The Bodhisattva acts as the guardian of the most profound vows, stationed in the deepest and darkest dimensions of the Six Realms, perhaps merely to ensure that not a single consciousness is ever entirely abandoned by the universe.
III. Avalokitesvara's Inquiry
When contemplating Ksitigarbha's vow, one must not overlook its profound connection to the Pure Land. According to the Ksitigarbha Sutra, the World-Honored One emitted boundless light from his crown and praised Ksitigarbha. At that moment, Avalokitesvara (Guanyin), the left attendant of Amitabha in the Pure Land, specifically rose to inquire about Ksitigarbha's inconceivable deeds. The intersection of these two great Bodhisattvas perhaps conceals a deeper meaning: Avalokitesvara is the vessel guiding beings to the Pure Land, while Ksitigarbha is the beacon eradicating obstacles in the Saha world. One guides to the nine-tiered lotus ponds, while the other pulls beings from the depths of hell. Their vows seemingly weave a network across the void, forging a flawless trajectory for beings to safely navigate from the burning house to the ultimate treasure.
IV. Mantra for Eradicating Fixed Karma
Within the Netherworld echoes a subtle sound capable of shattering karmic knots. According to the Dharani Collection Sutra, there exists a mantra widely known as the Mantra for Eradicating Fixed Karma. Its Sanskrit resonance, "Om pramardane svāhā," conceals the divine power to crush and scatter sins. This mantra is perhaps best viewed as a supportive tool for practitioners to uphold the Five Precepts and guard their minds daily. Whether or not one's karma is fully eradicated is a personal progression on the Bodhisattva path; yet, rebirth in the Pure Land relies purely on Amitabha's vow—the two do not conflict. The moment a practitioner awakens the supreme Bodhi mind and vows to be born in the Pure Land, a dedicated lotus seemingly blooms for them in the Seven-Jeweled Pond. Thereafter, the persistent eradication of karma is perhaps not to "exchange" for the ticket to the Pure Land, but merely a calm preparation of Bodhi provisions for future cultivation in the ultimate realm.
