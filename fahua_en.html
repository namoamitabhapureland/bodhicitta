Lotus Code ‧ The One Vehicle

Seven Metaphors and the Firehouse
SEVEN_METAPHORS_AND_FIREHOUSE
Saha is perhaps a massive firehouse burning with impermanence and suffering. To awaken slumbering beings, the World-Honored One compassionately laid out the "Seven Metaphors" in the Lotus Sutra. Viewing through the "Firehouse Metaphor": beings clad in carbon-based vessels run exhausted in the anxiety of life and death. When some practitioners saw through the agony of samsara and eagerly sought to cut the karmic connection, the Buddha manifested a safe server called the "Illusory City" (biased Nirvana) for them to rest. However, this city is merely a relay station. Settling here, thinking it is the ultimate end of the cosmos, leads to a stagnant loop of "neither advancing nor saving others."
True Reality and the Human Vessel
TRUE_REALITY_AND_HUMAN_VESSEL
When a practitioner's mind is thoroughly cleansed, the ultimate code of the Dharma Realm unlocks: "In the Buddha-lands of the ten directions, there is only the Dharma of the One Vehicle." This is the underlying logic of the universe—True Reality. All phenomena seem to arise and cease, but their essence is empty, luminous, and eternally abiding.

The sutra says "all beings can attain Buddhahood," pointing to the Tathagata nature inherent in all. Yet, from a practical system perspective, the "Human Vessel" is the only standard carrier in the Dharma Realm capable of executing the "Buddhahood Program". Beings in the three evil realms suffer endlessly, while celestial beings drown in pleasure, both unable to generate the mind of renunciation. Beings in other realms must cultivate through eons to switch their vessel to a "Human" one before they can truly activate their awareness and march toward the treasure of Buddhahood. A human life is as rare as a blind turtle finding a floating log; how can we not cherish this lifetime?
Universal Compassion and Original Teacher
AVALOKITESVARA_NETWORK_AND_SHAKYAMUNI
If Buddhahood is the only treasure, how do we safely arrive? The Lotus Sutra not only grants the "Great White Ox Cart" that leads straight to the treasure, but also reveals in the "Universal Gateway" chapter the cosmic network of Avalokitesvara Bodhisattva's compassionate responses, pointing distantly to Amitabha of Western Ultimate Bliss.

Therefore, when a practitioner sincerely recites each "Amitabha Buddha", an infinite sense of gratitude shall arise within the heart. Let us always remember: It is because our Original Teacher Shakyamuni Buddha renounced His infinite lifespan and descended into this firehouse to proclaim the "Pure Land Code" for us, that wandering beings have found the navigation to the Treasure. While being grateful for Amitabha's reception, we should also revere the Original Teacher's grace in transmitting the Dharma. We seek rebirth in Ultimate Bliss to exchange for the flawless golden vessel of the Great White Ox Cart, precisely to inherit our Original Teacher's great vow and return to the firehouse to save all beings.
Emergence of Many Treasures and Time Folding
MANY_TREASURES_AND_TIME_FOLDING
When a practitioner truly generates the Bodhicitta of "turning from the small to the great," declaring that the sole goal is Buddhahood, the most profound resonance in the Dharma Realm is triggered. Hidden deep within the multi-dimensional universe, the 500-yojana-tall "Stupa of Many Treasures" emerges from the earth and suspends in the void. Many Treasures Tathagata, who entered Nirvana long ago, and the currently preaching Shakyamuni Buddha, sit side by side as the stupa doors open.

This scene instantly shatters the illusory delusion of linear time and space. With extreme visual impact, it verifies to the practitioner: no matter how many Asamkhyeya kalpas have passed or how many billions of galaxies separate us, the truth of the "One Vehicle" remains eternally unmoved and vivid.
Infinite Dharmakaya and All Phenomena as Light
DHARMAKAYA_AND_INFINITE_LIGHT
Insightful to the reality of the Stupa, the practitioner will ultimately comprehend the profound secret of the "Lifespan of the Tathagata" chapter. Our Original Teacher Shakyamuni Buddha is by no means merely the awakened one who attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree 2,500 years ago. The sutra states: "Since I attained Buddhahood, an immeasurably long time has passed. My lifespan is countless Asamkhyeya kalpas, eternally abiding and never perishing."

The physical Nirvana is merely an "exit mechanism (expedient manifestation)" displayed to awaken beings. The true Dharmakaya Buddha (Vairocana) has no form and no bounds of lifespan. The Dharmakaya is actually one with all phenomena; all are the eternal, imperishable "pure light" of the Land of Eternally Tranquil Light. All mountains, rivers, earth, stars, and dust are refractions and manifestations of this light. When we illuminate our minds and see our true nature, we can behold this light of universal origin and endlessly reunite with all Buddhas in the void.
