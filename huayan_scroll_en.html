The Magnificent World Ocean ‧ A Drop of the Dharma

The ocean of Dharma is vast and profound, fully comprehensible only by Buddhas. As a lay practitioner, relying on logical teachings and the commentaries of past masters, this text attempts to explore a glimpse of the Tathagata's true meaning. Any omissions or errors are due to personal limitations. May the Three Jewels bestow their blessings, and may fellow practitioners share in this understanding.

[Vol I] Boundless Light Dharmakaya & Macro-Micro Unity

The Avatamsaka Sutra, known as the "King of Sutras," was revealed by Shakyamuni Buddha to great Bodhisattvas in deep samadhi immediately after his enlightenment under the Bodhi tree.

Its core reveals the ultimate reality of the "One True Dharma Realm, where all phenomena are mutually unhindered." Ordinary beings often consider the visible, vast starry sky as "macro," unaware that all visible forms are illusory aggregates of causes and conditions. They consider unseen dust particles as "micro," yet the formless realm contains the truest essence of the Dharma Realm.

"One is all, all is one. A mustard seed contains Mount Sumeru; a single thought is eternity."

The Avatamsaka Sutra uses the number "10" to symbolize the boundlessness and perfection of the Dharma Realm. This infinitely vast universe is an manifestation of the pure mind of the "Boundless Light Dharmakaya ‧ Vairocana Buddha." Every speck of dust and Buddha-land is a profound outflow of the Tathagata's Dharma body.

It is here that Shakyamuni Buddha guides stubborn beings in the "Saha World" of the five turbidities; simultaneously, within this exact same dimension, Amitabha Buddha established the "Land of Ultimate Bliss" (Pure Land) as an ultimate waystation for beings to transcend the three realms.

[Vol III] The 53 Visits & Guidance of the Two Sages

Vast as the Avatamsaka World is, without "Vows and Practices," one cannot enter. In the "Entering the Dharma Realm Chapter," Sudhana (The Youth of Good Wealth) initiates 53 visits to great spiritual teachers in his quest for supreme Bodhi, symbolizing the 53 stages of mind transformation from an ordinary being to Buddhahood.

However, the ultimate peak of Avatamsaka is not suspended in the void; it has a most real destination! When Sudhana reaches the highest realms of Bodhisattvas Manjushri and Samantabhadra, both great sages, without prior agreement, unanimously direct the ultimate fruition of Avatamsaka toward Amitabha's Land of Ultimate Bliss:

Manjushri's Vow Sutra:
"May I, at the end of my life, clear away all obstacles, see Amitabha face to face, and be born in the Land of Peace and Bliss."

Avatamsaka Sutra ‧ Samantabhadra's Vows:
"When the time of my death approaches, may all obstacles be destroyed, may I see Amitabha Buddha, and instantly be born in His Peaceful Land."

[Vol IV] Buddha's True Intent ‧ The Pure Land Closure

The zenith of the Avatamsaka Sutra is "The Ten Great Vows of Samantabhadra." Where exactly are these endless vows fulfilled? The teachings of Shakyamuni Buddha are perfectly consistent from beginning to end. The true answer has long been engraved in Amitabha's 22nd Great Vow in the Infinite Life Sutra:

"Those born in my land will ultimately reach the stage of becoming a Buddha in one lifetime... They will all perfectly cultivate the virtues of Bodhisattva Samantabhadra."

The Saha world holds too many conditions for regression, making it extremely difficult to enter the Avatamsaka state. Thus, Amitabha Buddha, out of great compassion, established the Pure Land. This realm serves as a "Waystation for the Bodhisattva Path." Once born there, one attains non-retrogression and possesses immeasurable spiritual powers, seamlessly fulfilling Samantabhadra's vast vows.

This digital dojo is established to help practitioners preview the nature of the Pure Land. A single connection through Ten Recitations places you conceptually in the 13th Tier of Avatamsaka; continuous pure mindfulness is walking the Bodhi path of Sudhana's 53 visits.

Practice Samantabhadra's Vows ‧ Return to Amitabha's Pure Land. Following the logic of the sutras, this is perhaps the ultimate original intent of Shakyamuni Buddha's appearance in this world—to guide us back to our true spiritual home.
